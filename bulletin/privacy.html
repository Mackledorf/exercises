Privacy Policy

Bulletin  ·  Last updated: April 20, 2026

Bulletin is an experimental student project built as a class exercise. It is not a commercial product. This privacy policy describes how the application handles user data in its current, non-production state.

What this app is

Bulletin is a personal visual pin-board web application. It lets users save images, organize them into boards, and optionally import boards from third-party services such as Are.na and Pinterest. The app is built and maintained by a single student as part of a design/development course and is not affiliated with any company or organization.

Data we collect

  • Account credentials — email address and a hashed password, used solely to authenticate you.
  • Board and pin data — names, descriptions, image URLs, and positions that you explicitly create inside the app.
  • OAuth tokens — when you connect a third-party service (e.g. Pinterest), an access token and refresh token are stored server-side in a private database row scoped to your account. These tokens are never exposed to other users or transmitted to any party other than the originating service.

Data we do not collect

  • We do not collect analytics, telemetry, or usage tracking of any kind.
  • We do not collect payment information.
  • We do not sell or share any data with third parties.
  • We do not run advertisements.

Third-party services

Bulletin uses Supabase for authentication and database storage. When you choose to connect your Pinterest account, your interaction with Pinterest's OAuth flow is governed by the Pinterest Privacy Policy. Bulletin only requests read-only access (boards:read, pins:read) and does not post, modify, or delete any Pinterest content on your behalf.

Data retention

Your data is retained for as long as your account exists. You may delete your account at any time, which will permanently remove all associated boards, pins, and OAuth tokens from our database.

Contact

This is a student project with no formal support channel. If you have questions, you can reach out to the developer directly through the course or class in which this project was submitted.
